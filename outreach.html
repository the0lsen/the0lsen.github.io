Theo Olsen Outreach

Science is best when it's shared

I am involved in several science outreach and community activities at Vassar College and beyond. I believe that making astronomy accessible to people outside the research community is an important part of being a scientist.

Activities

How I Engage

🔭
Observatory Host
Vassar College Observatory Open Nights
Vassar College · Poughkeepsie, NY · 2023–Present

I help host weekly public observing sessions at the Vassar College Observatory, where I teach students, faculty, and community members about the night sky. I also participate in the TESS Follow-Up Observing Program (TFOP), contributing ground-based photometric observations of planet candidates from NASA's TESS mission to help distinguish transiting planets from astrophysical false positives.

📰
Co-President & Founder
Physics & Astronomy Journal Club
Vassar College · 2024–Present

I co-lead the Vassar Physics & Astronomy Journal Club, which runs a weekly paper discussion group and a campus-wide public lecture series on advances in physics and astronomy. The club is open to students at all levels.

〰️
Creator
Fourier Wave Visualizer
Independent Project · 2025–Present

An interactive tool that turns musical chords into Fourier epicycles — linked circles whose combined motion draws waveforms in real time. Play notes on a piano keyboard, hear them synthesized, and explore how harmony looks and sounds across historical tuning systems from equal temperament to Pythagorean and meantone.

🪐
Creator
Gravitational Simulator Teaching Tool
Independent Project · 2024–Present

I am building an interactive physics puzzle game based on a gravitational simulator. Players adjust masses, velocities, and orbital parameters to solve a series of challenges, with the goal of building intuition for orbital mechanics and gravitational physics. The game is aimed at general audiences.

🎵
Creator
Orbital Resonance Music Generator
Independent Project · 2025–Present

I am developing a web-based n-body gravitational simulator that generates music from orbital dynamics. Each orbit produces rhythmic or harmonic events, making the patterns of resonant planetary systems audible in real time. I am very interested in the intersection of astrophysics and music, please reach out if you're interested in collaborating!

Research Representative
Physics & Astronomy Majors Committee
Vassar College · 2025–Present

I serve as the Research Representative on the Physics & Astronomy Majors Committee, where I represent student interests on matters related to research opportunities, departmental resources, and academic policy. I work to improve undergraduate access to research positions, particularly for students earlier in their academic careers.
